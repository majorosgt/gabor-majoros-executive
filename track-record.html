Track Record

Selected mandates and executive scope.

Governance complexity across international organisations does not follow a linear biography. What follows is a sector-led view of the mandates, governance challenges, and jurisdictional scope that define this profile — not a chronological resume.

Travel and Hospitality

Current · Global

Leading the group privacy and AI compliance function for one of the world's largest privately held travel organisations — operating across EMEA, the Americas, and Asia. Mandates in this sector have involved building global governance models from the ground up, establishing board-level accountability for AI and privacy risk, directing supervisory authority strategy, and managing a cross-functional international team. The scope encompasses both operational governance and the strategic conversations that sit above it.

Aviation

30+ Countries · Regulatory Engagement

Leading privacy strategy and governance design for a major European airline with regulatory exposure across more than thirty countries. This mandate required building the organisation's first enterprise privacy program, establishing cross-border data governance under multiple national frameworks, and representing the organisation before data protection authorities in investigation and enforcement-facing processes. The jurisdictional complexity and volume of data subjects involved demanded disciplined executive positioning throughout.

Financial Services

Banking · Supervisory Audits · Risk

Designing and implementing the privacy governance program for a major national banking group, operating across multiple financial entities under prudential regulatory oversight. Work in this sector encompassed supervisory audit management, personal data breach strategy, alignment with sector-specific risk and compliance structures, and advisory to executive leadership on regulatory posture. Financial services governance demands a precision that informs the broader advisory practice.

Legal and Regulatory Advisory

Multinational Clients · Contentious Matters

Over eight years of practice leading strategic compliance, technology, and regulatory advisory for multinational clients at a major professional services firm. This period established the foundation for complex stakeholder management, contentious privacy and employment matters, cross-border regulatory strategy, and the kind of legal-commercial judgment that distinguishes executive advisory from operational compliance. Clients spanned financial services, technology, energy, and retail sectors.

Executive scope across four sectors
The through-line

Governance design, authority strategy, and cross-border execution.

Across travel, aviation, banking, and advisory, the recurring pattern is the same: organisations facing regulatory complexity that exceeds standard legal counsel, requiring an executive who understands both the governance architecture and the board-level stakes.

The profile is strongest where AI governance, privacy, and regulatory risk must work together under international scrutiny — and where the right answer requires judgment, not only procedure.

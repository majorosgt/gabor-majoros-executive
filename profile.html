Profile

The arc.

From advisory to authority. From counsel to accountability.

Careers in governance are rarely linear. The ones that matter are built through accumulation — of responsibility, of pressure, of decisions that carried real consequences.

What follows is not a chronology. It is a record of how expertise compounds — and what that looks like at the point where law, technology, and executive accountability converge.

The journey

Three chapters. One consistent direction.

2011 – 2019 · Eight years

Deloitte Legal

Managing Associate · Head of Employment, Compliance & Technology

It began in advisory — but not as a follower of frameworks. The Employment, Compliance and Technology practice group did not exist before. It was built: the methodology, the client relationships, the team, the market-facing identity in data privacy and compliance.

Over eight years, the practice advised major multinational clients across technology, finance, healthcare, and retail. More than thirty GDPR implementation projects led directly — each one a different organisation, a different risk profile, a different set of trade-offs between regulatory obligation and operational reality.

This period established something that cannot be learned from a single mandate: the ability to structure a governance function from first principles — and to know what holds under pressure, because it was tested across the full range of real client environments.

2019 – 2022 · Three years

Banking & Aviation

Group Data Protection Officer

The move in-house was deliberate. Advisory expertise creates frameworks. Executive accountability tests them.

In banking — Hungary's largest banking group, thirty-plus subsidiaries across finance and fintech — the work meant designing and directing a privacy governance model under financial regulation overlay, conducting supervisory audits, managing breach investigations, and liaising directly with the national banking authority.

In aviation — one of Europe's fastest-growing airlines at the time — the mandate expanded geographically and in operational complexity. Thirty-plus countries. EEA, UK, UAE, third-country transfers. The first group-wide privacy framework for the airline, built and operationalised. Regulatory engagements, enforcement proceedings, and direct representation before data protection authorities across multiple jurisdictions.

This period established the capacity to operate at executive level, under enforcement pressure, in organisations where privacy failure carries direct legal and reputational consequence.

2022 – Present

Global Cruise Industry

Global Head of Privacy · AI Lead · Group Data Protection Officer

The current mandate operates at a different scale. One of the world's largest private companies — global operations spanning EMEA, the Americas, and Asia. The privacy and AI compliance function was built from the ground up: governance model, global Privacy Champion network, privacy-by-design integration across the entire organisation, and alignment with ESG, cybersecurity, and ethics programs.

Direct reporting line to the CEO and Executive Chairman. Leading a cross-functional team of ten privacy professionals across three continents. Managing the privacy program budget. Serving as primary interface with supervisory authorities across multiple jurisdictions — including enforcement-facing engagements, authority audits, and breach notification processes on six continents.

In parallel: building the AI governance function. EU AI Act implementation, high-risk AI system classification, accountability structure design, and board-level oversight frameworks — at an organisation that operates AI systems across every major market it serves.

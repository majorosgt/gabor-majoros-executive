Areas of Focus

Selected areas of professional depth.

01

AI Governance & AI Act

AI governance introduces a new class of enterprise risk.

This work focuses on structuring governance models aligned with the EU AI Act and emerging global frameworks, including risk classification, accountability structures, and executive oversight.

The objective is not compliance alone — but strategic risk alignment and defensibility.

02

Privacy Strategy & Operating Model

Privacy programs require alignment between legal expectation, operational execution, and risk ownership.

Designing scalable operating models ensures that governance is embedded into decision-making, rather than existing as a parallel compliance layer.

03

Cross-Border Regulatory Complexity

Cross-border operations introduce compounded regulatory risk.

This work focuses on governance architectures that maintain coherence where legal frameworks diverge and operational alignment becomes critical.

04

Authority-Facing Matters

Regulatory engagement is inherently strategic.

Positioning, narrative, and timing define outcomes alongside legal analysis, particularly in high-scrutiny environments.

05

Contentious Privacy Matters

In high-risk scenarios, privacy becomes a question of institutional posture.

Aligning legal argument, governance structures, and organisational credibility is essential.

06

Board & Executive Governance

At executive level, privacy and AI are not compliance topics. They are strategic risk decisions.

The role is to translate regulatory complexity into structured judgment that supports accountable decision-making.

Executive Focus

Selected areas of professional depth.

Six areas of deep professional expertise, built across 15 years of international practice in AI governance, privacy, regulatory strategy, and executive leadership — spanning travel, aviation, financial services, and legal-regulatory environments.

01

AI Governance and Compliance

Organisations introducing AI systems face a rapidly evolving regulatory landscape. Core professional depth here includes governance model design, high-risk AI categorisation, internal accountability structures, and readiness for the EU AI Act. The goal is not compliance for its own sake — it is building governance that earns trust at board level and holds up under external scrutiny.

02

Privacy Strategy and Operating Model Design

Privacy governance extends well beyond legal obligations. This professional depth covers enterprise-wide privacy operating model design — defining accountability structures, embedding privacy into product and operational workflows, structuring cross-border data governance, and building programs that scale as the organisation grows. The focus is executive coherence, not procedural overhead.

03

Cross-Border Risk and Regulatory Complexity

Organisations operating across multiple jurisdictions face compounding regulatory obligations that rarely resolve neatly. This expertise spans cross-border risk mapping, jurisdictional conflict analysis, and the governance architecture needed to remain coherent across Europe, the Americas, and Asia — particularly in sectors where regulatory expectations are both high and divergent.

04

Strategic Handling of Authority-Facing Matters

Interactions with data protection authorities, AI regulators, and other supervisory bodies require a disciplined blend of legal positioning, operational credibility, and executive tone. Professional experience here encompasses investigation strategy, authority engagement, escalation management, and the preparation required before, during, and after enforcement-sensitive situations.

05

Privacy-Related Contentious and Regulatory Matters

When privacy disputes become contentious — whether with regulators, data subjects, or counterparties — the strategic framing matters as much as the legal argument. This experience spans complex regulatory matters, coordinating across legal, compliance, and business leadership to reach outcomes that protect both rights and institutional standing.

06

Board and Executive-Level Governance

At board level, AI governance and privacy are no longer technical matters — they are strategic risks and reputational considerations. This professional focus is on translating regulatory complexity into clear executive framing, equipping boards for informed decision-making, and ensuring that the governance posture reflects and reinforces the organisation's broader position.
